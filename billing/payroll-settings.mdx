# Payroll Setup & Management

Kenko provides a robust Payroll system that allows you to automate compensation, manage pay periods, and ensure your staff is paid accurately and on time. This guide covers running payroll, templates, settings, and payroll history.

## Payroll Overview

The Payroll module is divided into four main areas

- **Run Payroll**
- **Payroll History**
- **Payroll Templates**
- **Payroll Settings**

Each section is designed to make your payroll management easy and compliant.

## ![Screenshot2025 04 28at11 58 03AM Pn](/images/Screenshot2025-04-28at11.58.03AM.png)

Run Payroll

Running payroll is the final step where all staff compensations are calculated and processed.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select Pay Period">
    Navigate to `Payroll > Run Payroll`.\
    Choose the pay period you want to finalize payroll for.

    ![Screenshot2025 04 28at12 00 52PM Pn](/images/Screenshot2025-04-28at12.00.52PM.png)

    ![Screenshot2025 04 28at11 59 19AM Pn](/images/Screenshot2025-04-28at11.59.19AM.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review Draft Payroll">
    Check each staff’s

    - Total hours/classes
    - Bonuses, adjustments, and deductions
    - Deductions, Gross Pay and Reimbursements and Fixed Pay

    ![Screenshot2025 04 28at12 12 45PM Pn](/images/Screenshot2025-04-28at12.12.45PM.png)

    You can also edit it for an individual staff as follows. ( Editable items are Bonus, Tips, Other earnings, Deductions and Reimbusement )

    ![Screenshot2025 04 28at12 14 11PM Pn](/images/Screenshot2025-04-28at12.14.11PM.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit Payroll">
    Once finalized, Run the payroll. Once you make the payment to yout Staff and Instructors, you can record the payment date and Export the details for your records.

    <Note>
      Once submitted, it moves from **Draft** ➔ **Finalized** status.
    </Note>
    <Warning>
      Kenko does not record the payment and bank details of Staffs. You have to use a trhird party software like Quickbooks or Gusto to process the payment to your staff.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Payroll cannot be submitted until the pay period has ended to ensure complete and accurate data capture.
</Info>

## Payroll Templates

Templates standardize how compensation is calculated.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Templates">
    Go to `Payroll > Payroll Templates` and click `Create Template`.

    ![Screenshot2025 04 28at12 33 05PM Pn](/images/Screenshot2025-04-28at12.33.05PM.png)

    Add name and description for better identification eventually.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Template Type">
    Choose based on staff role and the services they are offering

    <AccordionGroup>
      <Accordion title="Fixed Salary" icon="money-bill">
        Set a fixed weekly or monthly amount for staff, regardless of the number of hours, classes, or appointments.

        **Use Case**\
        Ideal for full-time or part-time employees on a consistent schedule, such as Studio Managers or Administrative roles.

        ![Screenshot2025 04 28at12 39 35PM Pn](/images/Screenshot2025-04-28at12.39.35PM.png)
      </Accordion>
      <Accordion title="Class Pay Rate" icon="school">
        Assign a flat rate, tiered or Flat tiered amount for every class an instructor teaches.

        | Pay Rate Model       | How Payment Works                                                       | Best Use Case                                      | Example Calculation                                                                                         |
        | -------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
        | **Flat Rate**        | Fixed amount paid **per booking**.                                      | Standard simple classes or 1:1 sessions.           | \$20 per booking × 5 bookings → **\$100 total**                                                             |
        | **Tiered Rate**      | Different **rate per booking** based on booking volume thresholds.      | Encouraging instructors to bring in more bookings. | First 5 bookings @ \$10 each, next 5 bookings @ \$15 each → **(5×\$10) \+ (5×\$15) = \$125 total**          |
        | **Flat Tiered Rate** | **Single flat amount** based on total bookings range (not per booking). | High-volume performance bonuses.                   | 20 bookings qualifies for \$300 payout flat → **\$300 total** (NOT multiplied by 20 bookings individually). |

        <info>
  In **Tiered**, you pay for **each** booking at its respective rate.\
  In **Flat Tiered**, you pay **once** based on where total bookings fall — **NOT per booking**.
</info>

        **Use Case**\
        Best suited for Yoga, Pilates, or Group Fitness instructors where payment is tied to classes delivered.

        ![Screenshot2025 04 28at12 41 11PM Pn](/images/Screenshot2025-04-28at12.41.11PM.png)
      </Accordion>
      <Accordion title="Course Pay Rate" icon="book">
        Define a payment type for staff completing a full course or workshop series.

        | Pay Rate Model  | How Payment Works                                                    | Best Use Case                                    | Example Calculation                                                                                  |
        | --------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
        | **Flat Rate**   | Fixed amount paid **per course sale**.                               | Small fixed-value courses or internal workshops. | Instructor earns \$50 flat per course sold, regardless of sale price.                                |
        | **Percentage**  | Pays a **% of the course sale value**.                               | Varying course prices or incentivizing sales.    | Course sold for \$200 → 10% payout = **\$20 earned per sale**.                                       |
        | **Tiered Rate** | Pays a **different rate per sale based on sales volume** thresholds. | Motivating staff for higher enrollments.         | 1–10 sales: \$10/sale, 11–20 sales: \$15/sale → 15 sales = **(10×\$10) \+ (5×\$15) = \$175 earned**. |

        <info>
  **Flat** = same amount per course sold.

  **Percentage** = dynamic, based on course price.

  **Tiered** = progressive earnings increase with more sales.
</info>

        <tip>
  Combine "Pay per course session" and "Pay per sale" for flexible compensation!\
  (e.g., Instructor earns $20 per session + 10% per course sold.)
</tip>

        **Use Case**\
        Useful for seasonal or special event instructors running multi-session courses like Bootcamps or Retreats.

        ![Screenshot2025 04 28at12 50 25PM Pn](/images/Screenshot2025-04-28at12.50.25PM.png)
      </Accordion>
      <Accordion title="Appointment Pay Rate" icon="calendar-clock">
        Set payment for each appointment or 1:1 session completed.

        Appointment's template is same as of Class's payment template

        **Use Case**\
        Perfect for personal trainers, massage therapists, wellness consultants, and others offering appointment-based services.

        ![Screenshot2025 04 28at12 55 10PM Pn](/images/Screenshot2025-04-28at12.55.10PM.png)
      </Accordion>
      <Accordion title="Hourly Pay Rate" icon="clock">
        Pay staff according to the number of hours worked or tracked.

        **Use Case**\
        Ideal for flexible roles such as substitute teachers, front-desk assistants, or cleaning/maintenance crews.

        ![Screenshot2025 04 28at12 56 44PM Pn](/images/Screenshot2025-04-28at12.56.44PM.png)
      </Accordion>
    </AccordionGroup>
    <Info>
      It's common to assign a **combination of these compensation types** to a single staff member.\
      (e.g., a staff member may receive a fixed salary plus per-class bonuses.)
    </Info>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure Pay Rates">
    Define base rates, overtime multipliers, and bonus eligibility. Choose whether to add late cancellations ( Staff will still get paid incase the Customer decides to cancel late )
  </Step>
  <Step title="Apply Templates">
    Assign templates to individual staff profiles or roles.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Hourly pay rate templates automatically calculate based on logged hours during the selected pay period.
</Info>

## Setting Staff Compensation

Compensation settings control individual payout configurations.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Staff Profile">
    Navigate to `Contacts > Staff > Compensation Settings`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Attach Payroll Template">
    Link the appropriate payroll template (Hourly, Per Class, Fixed).

    <AccordionGroup>
      <Accordion title="Fixed Salary (Weekly or Monthly)" icon="calendar">
        Assign a fixed salary amount to staff, either on a weekly or monthly basis.

        - Ideal for full-time or part-time employees with a consistent workload.
        - Salary is calculated automatically for each pay period.
        - Does not vary based on hours, classes, or bookings.
      </Accordion>
      <Accordion title="Hourly Pay" icon="clock">
        Pay staff based on the number of hours they work.

        - Staff members can clock in/out or have hours logged manually.
        - Payroll will calculate total hours multiplied by the hourly rate.
        - Useful for flexible roles like front desk managers or cleaners.
      </Accordion>
      <Accordion title="Pay per Class" icon="graduation-cap">
        Compensate staff based on the number of classes they teach.

        - Set a fixed amount for each class type or session.
        - Ideal for fitness instructors, yoga teachers, etc.
        - Each taught class triggers an automatic earning entry.
      </Accordion>
      <Accordion title="Pay per Course" icon="chalkboard">
        Staff are paid a set amount per completed course they deliver.

        - Works best for longer multi-session workshops or academies.
        - One payout per course, regardless of the number of sessions.
        - Encourages end-to-end delivery responsibility.
      </Accordion>
      <Accordion title="Pay per Appointment" icon="calendar-days">
        Pay staff individually for each appointment completed.

        - Applicable for services like personal training, massages, consultations.
        - Amount earned is tied to each successful appointment.
        - Can be configured separately for different appointment types.
      </Accordion>
    </AccordionGroup>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Custom Overrides (Optional)">
    You can override default template values for specific staff members.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Staff compensation changes will take effect starting the next open pay period.
</Note>

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Why can't I run my payroll before the pay period ends?" icon="question-mark-circle">
    To ensure complete data integrity (attendance, hours, sales), payrolls are locked until the pay cycle is complete.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How does Hourly Pay calculate in payroll?" icon="calculator">
    Kenko automatically multiplies the hourly rate by the total logged hours in the period and applies overtime if configured.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I edit a payroll after submission?" icon="pencil">
    Only drafts can be edited. Once submitted, you must void and recreate if corrections are needed.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
