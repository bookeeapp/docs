# Payment Methods

Offering flexible payment options is essential for providing a smooth experience for your members. Kenko supports multiple payment methods and helps you manage them securely.

## Supported Payment Methods

Kenko allows you to accept various payment types:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Credit & Debit Cards" icon="credit-card">
    Accept all major credit and debit cards including Visa, Mastercard, American Express, and Discover.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Bank Transfers (ACH)" icon="university">
    Direct transfers from members' bank accounts for recurring payments (US only).
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Digital Wallets" icon="wallet">
    Support for Apple Pay, Google Pay, and other mobile payment options.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Manual Payments" icon="hand-holding-usd">
    Record cash, check, or other offline payment methods.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Setting Up Payment Processing

Before accepting payments, you need to configure your payment processing settings.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access Payment Settings">
    Navigate to **Admin Settings** > **Billing & Payments** > **Payment Processing**.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Connect Payment Processor">
    Choose and connect your payment processor:
    - Stripe (recommended)
    - PayPal
    - Square
    - Other supported processors
    
    Follow the integration steps for your chosen processor.
    
    <Note>
      See our [Stripe Connection Guide](../../integrations/stripe/account-connection) for detailed setup instructions for our recommended processor.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Configure Payment Settings">
    Set your payment preferences:
    - Default payment methods to accept
    - Auto-billing settings
    - Failed payment handling
    - Receipt and invoice customization
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set Up Payment Security">
    Configure security settings:
    - CVV requirements
    - Address verification
    - Fraud detection levels
    - PCI compliance settings
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Test Your Configuration">
    Run a test transaction to ensure everything is working properly:
    1. Enable test mode
    2. Process a test payment
    3. Verify it appears correctly in your payment processor dashboard
    4. Disable test mode when complete
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Managing Member Payment Methods

### Adding Payment Methods for Members

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion icon="user-plus" title="During Member Registration">
    When creating a new member account:
    
    1. Navigate to the payment section of the new member form
    2. Select **Add Payment Method**
    3. Choose the payment type
    4. Enter the payment details or have the member enter them directly
    5. Set as default payment method if appropriate
    6. Complete the registration process
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion icon="user-edit" title="For Existing Members">
    To add a payment method to an existing profile:
    
    1. Open the member's profile
    2. Go to the **Billing** tab
    3. Click **Add Payment Method**
    4. Choose the payment type
    5. Enter the required details
    6. Set as default if desired
    7. Save the new payment method
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion icon="user-check" title="Member Self-Service">
    Members can add their own payment methods through the member portal:
    
    1. Member logs into their account
    2. Navigates to **Account Settings** > **Payment Methods**
    3. Selects **Add Payment Method**
    4. Enters their payment information
    5. Sets default preferences
    
    <Note>
      The member portal uses secure forms that meet PCI compliance requirements for handling payment information.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Managing Multiple Payment Methods

Members can have multiple payment methods on file:

1. The designated **default** method is used for automatic payments
2. Members can choose which payment method to use for one-time purchases
3. Default methods can be changed at any time:
   - By staff through the member profile
   - By members through their account portal

## Handling Failed Payments

Kenko provides automated tools to help manage payment failures.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Configure Retry Settings">
    Navigate to **Admin Settings** > **Billing & Payments** > **Failed Payment Handling**.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set Retry Schedule">
    Configure when and how often to retry failed payments:
    - Initial retry timeframe (typically 1-3 days)
    - Number of retry attempts
    - Interval between attempts
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Configure Notifications">
    Set up automated communications:
    - Failed payment notifications
    - Retry notifications
    - Final payment reminder before account suspension
    
    <Note>
      Customize notification templates in **Admin Settings** > **Communications** > **Email Templates**.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Define Account Actions">
    Determine what happens after multiple failed attempts:
    - When to suspend membership access
    - Whether to automatically cancel after a certain period
    - Special handling for long-term members
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Manual Payment Resolution

For handling payment issues that require intervention:

1. Navigate to **Billing** > **Failed Payments**
2. Review the list of pending payment issues
3. Select a member to contact or resolve manually
4. Choose an action:
   - Contact member
   - Process a different payment method
   - Apply a credit or adjustment
   - Record a manual payment
   - Create a payment plan
