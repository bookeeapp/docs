# How not to send spammy emails 101

Email marketing is a powerful way to connect with your audience, but getting your emails into inboxes—not spam folders—is the real challenge. This guide covers key strategies to ensure your emails get noticed and engaged with.

<CardGroup cols={1}>
  <Card title="Content Relevance" icon="user">
    Ensure your email content is relevant and tailored to your audience. Avoid spammy keywords such as "earn," "sale," or "cash," which Gmail’s filters often flag.
  </Card>
  <Card title="User Engagement" icon="users">
    Poor engagement metrics like low open rates can trigger Gmail’s spam filters. Analyze your campaigns regularly to improve engagement.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Contact List Management" icon="table">
    Sudden, drastic increases in your contact list may signal spamming behavior. Build your list organically and avoid purchasing email lists.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Sending Frequency" icon="mobile-signal">
    Sending emails too frequently can annoy recipients and increase the likelihood your messages are marked as spam.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<CardGroup cols={1}>
  <Card title="Sender Name and Subject Line" icon="voicemail">
    Use recognizable sender names and relevant subject lines to increase open rates and avoid spam flags.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Content Formatting" icon="monitor-waveform">
    Keep your email design simple. Avoid excessive HTML, URLs, or heavy media files that Gmail associates with promotional content.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Call-to-Action (CTA)" icon="bullseye-pointer">
    Craft clear, compelling CTAs with prominent, easily clickable links to boost user engagement.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Info>
  Following these guidelines helps optimize your email campaigns for spam filters, increasing your chances of landing in your audience’s primary inbox. Although no method is foolproof, careful planning and adherence to best practices significantly improve deliverability and engagement.
</Info>
