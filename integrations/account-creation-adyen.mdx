# Adyen Account Setup

Kenko partners with Adyen to provide secure, reliable payment processing for your fitness business. This guide will walk you through connecting your Adyen account to Kenko.

## Why Kenko Needs Adyen

Kenko integrates with Adyen to offer a seamless and secure payment experience. Adyen handles all aspects of payment processing, ensuring PCI-compliant transactions, offering global payment options, and simplifying recurring billing. By connecting Adyen, Kenko can manage payments efficiently, providing a smoother client experience and automated subscription management.

## Before You Begin

Before connecting Adyen to Kenko, ensure you have

1. An active Kenko account with administrative privileges.
2. An Adyen account (or be prepared to create one).
3. Business details including
   - Legal business name
   - Business address
   - Tax ID number
   - Banking information for deposits

<Note>
  If you don't already have an Adyen account, you'll be able to create one during the connection process.
</Note>

## Creating an Adyen Account

To create an Adyen account:

1. **Sign Up for a Test Account**: Visit [Adyen's Get Started page](https://docs.adyen.com/get-started-with-adyen/) and sign up for a free test account. This gives you access to the test Customer Area, where you can manage your integration across regions and currencies.
2. **Build Your Integration**: Start building your integration using your test account. Choose the integration that matches your business needs, such as online payments or in-person payments.
3. **Apply for a Live Account**: Once you're ready to go live, apply for a live account. You'll need to provide
   - Company registration documents
   - Proof of tax information or VAT information
   - Merchant bank statement (optional)
   - Proof of identity and proof of residency

   For more details, refer to [Adyen's application requirements](https://docs.adyen.com/get-started-with-adyen/application-requirements/).
4. **Sign the Contract**: After your application is approved, Adyen will send you a contract. Complete and return it to gain access to your live Customer Area.
5. **Configure Your Live Account**: Set up your live account settings, including payment methods, webhooks, and other configurations.

## Connection Process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access Payment Settings">
    In your Kenko dashboard, navigate to **Admin Settings** \> **Billing & Payments** \> **Payment Processing**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Adyen">
    In the Payment Processors section, click **Connect** next to the Adyen option.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Connection Type">
    Select one of the following options

    - **Connect existing Adyen account**: Link your current Adyen account.
    - **Create new Adyen account**: Set up a new Adyen account through Kenko.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize Connection">
    You'll be redirected to Adyen's website to

    1. Login to your existing Adyen account (or create a new one).
    2. Review the permissions Kenko is requesting.
    3. Authorize the connection.

    <Warning>
      Ensure you're granting access to the correct Adyen account, especially if you manage multiple businesses.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete Account Setup">
    If this is a new Adyen account, you'll need to provide additional information

    - Business details and address
    - Banking information for deposits
    - Identity verification documents

    Existing accounts may already have this information, but verify it's correct.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure Payment Settings">
    After connecting, return to Kenko to configure your payment settings

    1. Default payment methods to accept
    2. Automatic billing retry settings
    3. Payment failure notifications
    4. Receipt and invoice customization
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Testing Your Connection

After connecting Adyen, we recommend testing the integration

1. Go to **Admin Settings** \> **Billing & Payments** \> **Test Mode**
2. Toggle **Test Mode** to ON
3. Perform a test transaction using Adyen's [test card numbers](https://docs.adyen.com/development-resources/test-cards/test-card-numbers)
   - Test successful payment: 4111 1111 1111 1111
   - Test failed payment: 4000 0000 0000 0002
4. Verify the test transaction appears in both Kenko and your Adyen dashboard
5. Toggle **Test Mode** OFF when finished testing

<Warning>
  Always remember to turn off Test Mode before processing real transactions.
</Warning>

## Managing Your Adyen Connection

### Viewing Connected Account

To view your connected Adyen account

1. Go to **Admin Settings** \> **Billing & Payments** \> **Payment Processing**
2. Look for the "Connected Account" section to see which Adyen account is connected

### Disconnecting Adyen

If you need to disconnect your Adyen account

1. Go to **Admin Settings** \> **Billing & Payments** \> **Payment Processing**
2. Click **Disconnect** next to your Adyen connection
3. Confirm the disconnection

<Warning>
  Disconnecting your Adyen account will prevent all future payments from processing. Only disconnect if you're switching payment processors or closing your account.
</Warning>

## Troubleshooting

If you encounter issues with your payment integrations, try these common solutions

- **Connection Issues**: Verify your API credentials are correct and up to date.
- **Failed Payments**: Check that the payment method information is valid and has sufficient funds.
- **Webhook Errors**: Ensure webhook endpoints are properly configured.
- **Currency Mismatches**: Confirm that you've enabled the correct currencies in both Kenko and your payment processor.

For additional assistance, contact [Kenko Support](mailto:support@gokenko.com).
